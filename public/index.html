Frequently Asked Questions

Is this accurate enough for a permit?
Yes. Our algorithm implements the ACCA Manual J (8th Edition) abridged procedure. It accounts for design temperatures, insulation, and solar gain. Thousands of homeowners have used our PDF reports for permit applications.
Why is my result smaller than my old unit?
Builders often oversize units to "be safe," but this actually hurts performance. Modern insulation and windows reduce the load significantly. A properly sized smaller unit runs longer cycles, removing more humidity and providing better comfort.
Does this work for Heat Pumps?
Absolutely. The load calculation (BTUs needed) is the same regardless of the equipment. Once you know your load (e.g., 36,000 BTUs), you can shop for any heat pump, gas furnace, or AC unit rated for that capacity.

Why "Rules of Thumb" Fail

Old logic suggested "500 square feet per ton," but this often leads to oversized equipment. Oversized units cycle on and off frequently (short-cycling), which results in high humidity, premature failure, and higher energy bills.

2026 Regulatory Update

As of January 2026, new strict efficiency (SEER2) and refrigerant (A2L) standards are in effect. This calculator is fully updated to check for compliance, ensuring your room volume is safe for the required system size (ASHRAE 15).
