Why "Rule of Thumb" Sizing is Costing You Thousands

By HVAC Engineer • Jan 22, 2026 • Efficiency

For decades, many HVAC contractors have used a simple trick: "500 square feet per ton." Got a 2,000 sq ft house? Install a 4-ton unit. Done. The problem? This method is almost always wrong, and it leaves homeowners with damp, moldy, and inefficient homes.

The Truth About Load Calculations

A house in Phoenix, AZ needs completely different cooling than an identical house in Minneapolis, MN. Insulation, window direction (solar gain), air leakage, and even the color of your roof affect the load.

The Dangers of Oversizing

Many people think "bigger is better." In HVAC, bigger is broken.

  • Short Cycling: A big unit cools the air too fast. It turns on, blasts cold air for 5 minutes, and shuts off. This wears out the compressor.
  • Humidity Issues: Air conditioners dehumidify by running for long cycles. If it shuts off too fast, the air gets cold but stays wet and clammy.
  • Mold Risk: That excess moisture settles in ducts, creating a breeding ground for mold.
