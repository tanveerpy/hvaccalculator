The Complete Guide to the 2026 SEER2 & A2L Refrigerant Transition

By Expert Technician • Jan 22, 2026 • Regulations

If you're planning to replace your HVAC system in 2026, you're stepping into a new era of heating and cooling. The EPA and DOE have enforced two major changes: higher efficiency standards (SEER2) and a mandatory shift to low-GWP refrigerants (A2L). This guide explains what you need to know.

What is SEER2?

SEER (Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio) has been the standard for decades. However, the old testing method didn't accurately reflect real-world conditions, specifically the high static pressure found in modern ductwork.

SEER2 is the new "M1" testing procedure. It increases the external static pressure by 5x during testing to simulate realistic bad ductwork. As a result, a unit that was "14 SEER" might only rate as "13.4 SEER2".

  • North Region: Minimum 13.4 SEER2 for ACs.
  • South/Southwest: Minimum 14.3 SEER2 for ACs.
  • Heat Pumps: Minimum 14.3 SEER2 national average.

The A2L Refrigerant Shift (R-454B)

The familiar R-410A (Puron) is being phased out due to its high Global Warming Potential (GWP). The replacement? A2L refrigerants like R-454B or R-32.

"A2L" stands for "Non-Toxic, Mildly Flammable." While that sounds scary, these refrigerants are extremely safe when installed correctly. However, new systems require:

  • Refrigerant Sensors: To detect leaks in indoor coils.
  • Charge Limits: Strict limits on how much refrigerant can be in a system based on the room size (which is why our calculator checks room volume!).
  • Spark-Proof Tools: Technicians need specialized equipment.

What This Means for Cost

Expect new systems to cost 15-25% more than pre-2025 models. This is due to the added sensors, more robust coils needed for SEER2 compliance, and the training required for A2L handling.

Need a Sizing Check?

With costs rising, sizing your unit correctly is critical. Don't pay for a 4-ton unit if you only need 3 tons.
