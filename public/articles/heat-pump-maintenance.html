5 Critical Warning Signs Your Heat Pump is Failing

By Service Lead • Jan 22, 2026 • Maintenance

Heat pumps are workhorses—they run all summer to cool you and all winter to heat you. That double-duty means they wear out faster than furnaces. Catching these signs early can save you from a complete compressor failure in the middle of January.

1. The "Aux Heat" Light Stays On

Your heat pump should only use auxiliary (strip) heat when it's extremely cold (below 30°F). If it's running in 40-50°F weather, your compressor isn't doing its job. This will TRIPLE your electric bill.

2. Strange Grinding Noises

Rattling is often a loose panel. But deep grinding? That's usually motor bearings failing. Screeching on startup can indicate a bad capacitor or compressor issue.

3. Icing Up in Summer

A heat pump should never have ice on the indoor coil in summer. This usually means one of two things:

  • Low Airflow: Your filter is completely clogged.
  • Refrigerant Leak: Low pressure causes coils to freeze. This requires a professional leak search.

4. Short Cycling

If your unit turns on and off every 3 minutes, it's overheating or the safety switches are tripping. This is the fastest way to kill a compressor.

Is It Time to Replace?

If repairs cost more than $1,500, check if a new SEER2 system makes sense for your home size.
